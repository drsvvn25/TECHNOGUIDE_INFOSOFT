## SECTION 1: PRODUCT DESCRIPTION PROMPTS

### Prompt 1.1: Eco-Friendly Product
```
Write a compelling product description for [PRODUCT NAME] (price: $[PRICE]) that:
- Targets eco-conscious consumers aged 25-45
- Opens with the sustainability angle
- Highlights these features: [FEATURE 1], [FEATURE 2], [FEATURE 3]
- Translates each feature into a lifestyle benefit
- Addresses the potential objection: "Is it more expensive because it's eco-friendly?"
- Uses these power words: sustainable, premium, conscious, mindful, impact
- Ends with an emotional call-to-action, not just "Buy Now"
- Tone: Inspiring but authentic, not preachy
- Length: 200-250 words
```

### Prompt 1.2: Luxury/Premium Product
```
Write an elegant product description for our luxury [PRODUCT TYPE] that:
- Uses sophisticated, refined vocabulary
- Target audience: Affluent professionals 35-60 who value quality and craftsmanship
- Emphasizes not just the product but the experience and status
- Opens with the aspirational benefit, not the product name
- Mentions: materials, craftsmanship, exclusivity
- Includes 2-3 lifestyle benefits (how it makes their life better)
- Uses subtle luxury language: "curated," "artisanal," "bespoke," "heritage," "refined"
- Avoids: superlatives, ALL CAPS, exclamation marks
- Tone: Sophisticated, understated confidence
- Length: 180-220 words
```

### Prompt 1.3: Budget-Friendly Product
```
Write a persuasive product description for [PRODUCT] (budget option, price: $[PRICE]) that:
- Target: Price-conscious shoppers who still want quality
- Main angle: Great value, no compromises on quality
- Open with: "You don't have to choose between price and quality"
- Highlight: [FEATURE 1], [FEATURE 2], [FEATURE 3]
- Compare positively (but fairly) to expensive alternatives
- Emphasize: Affordability + quality + practicality
- Use words: smart choice, smart spending, value-packed, efficient, practical, dependable
- Include customer benefit: What can they do with the money they save?
- Tone: Friendly, down-to-earth, no pretense
- Length: 150-200 words
```

### Prompt 1.4: Tech/Software Product
```
Write a product description for [TECH PRODUCT/SOFTWARE] that:
- Target: [USER TYPE - e.g., "small business owners," "freelancers," "developers"]
- Open with the main problem it solves
- Avoid: Excessive jargon, tech-speak that confuses
- Explain: 3-4 key features in plain English (what it does, why it matters)
- Highlight the benefit/outcome: What will they be able to do?
- Address ROI: How will this save them time/money/frustration?
- Include: Ease of use, support quality
- Use language: intuitive, seamless, powerful, efficient, automated
- Tone: Clear, helpful, confident
- Length: 200-300 words
```

### Prompt 1.5: Health/Wellness Product
```
Write a health-focused product description for [PRODUCT] that:
- Target audience: Health-conscious consumers aged [AGE RANGE]
- Main concern: Safety, efficacy, ingredients
- Open with: The benefit/result they'll experience
- Must include: Key ingredients/components and their benefits
- Mention: Third-party testing, certifications, scientific backing
- Address potential skepticism: "Does this really work?"
- Use words: proven, natural, pure, safe, tested, certified, clinical results
- Avoid: Medical claims, overpromising, unsubstantiated benefits
- Include: How to use, expected timeline for results
- Tone: Trustworthy, evidence-based, caring
- Length: 250-350 words
```

## SECTION 3: EMAIL MARKETING PROMPTS

### Prompt 3.1: Welcome Email
```
Write a welcome email for new customers to [BRAND/SERVICE] that:
- Subject line: Creates excitement and appreciation (under 50 characters)
  - Start with gratitude or welcome sentiment
  - Include one benefit or incentive hint
- Greeting: Personalized, warm ("Hey [Name]" or "Welcome!")
- Body structure:
  1. Thank them sincerely for joining
  2. Quick brand overview: What you do + why it matters (2-3 sentences)
  3. What they can expect from your emails
  4. Exclusive welcome offer: [SPECIFIC INCENTIVE - e.g., "15% off your first order"]
  5. Call-to-action: Clear button or link text
  6. Sign-off: Warm, personal closing
- Optional: Brief product/service highlights
- Tone: Warm, genuine, welcoming, not overly salesy
- Include: 1-2 product images or brand imagery description
- Footer: Unsubscribe link, contact info, social links
- Length: 100-150 words for body
```

### Prompt 3.2: Product Launch Email
```
Write a product launch announcement email for [NEW PRODUCT] that:
- Subject line: Creates excitement + urgency (under 55 characters)
  - Examples: "Introducing [Product]: Here's 20% off," "First look: [Product] is here"
- Greeting: "Hi [Name]" or "We can't wait to show you something special"
- Opening: Why you built this product (the problem it solves)
- Product description: 2-3 key features and how they help customer
- Visual: Product image or hero image description
- The offer: Early bird discount, exclusive launch pricing, or limited quantity
- FOMO element: "Available to subscribers first for 48 hours"
- CTA button: "Shop Now," "Get Yours," "Claim Your Early Bird Deal"
- Include: Customer testimonial/social proof if available
- Closing: Excitement statement + support contact info
- Tone: Excited, exclusive, helpful, not overly promotional
- Length: 150-200 words
```

### Prompt 3.3: Abandoned Cart Email
```
Write an abandoned cart recovery email that:
- Subject line: Gentle reminder + incentive (under 55 characters)
  - Options: "You left something behind," "5% off your cart," "Complete your order"
- Greeting: Casual, friendly
- Opening: "You left these items in your cart" (empathetic, not accusatory)
- Show: List the specific items abandoned (with images if possible)
- Reminder: Why they wanted these items (the benefit, not the feature)
- Offer: Incentive to complete purchase (e.g., "Use code SAVE10 for 10% off")
- Payment reassurance: "Secure checkout" / "Trusted by [customers]"
- Clear CTA button: "Complete My Purchase" or "Get My Items"
- Backup link: In case button doesn't work
- Closing: Something like "We're here to help if you have questions"
- Footer: Contact info, unsubscribe link
- Tone: Helpful, not pushy, understanding if they changed their mind
- Timeline suggestion: Send within 1 hour of abandonment
```

### Prompt 3.4: Newsletter Email
```
Write a monthly newsletter email for [BRAND] subscribers that:
- Subject line: Curiosity + value (under 50 characters)
  - Examples: "This month's best tips," "What's trending this month," "Exclusive content inside"
- Header: Eye-catching section teasing top content
- Structure (3-5 sections):
  1. Featured article/tip: [TOPIC] with 2-3 sentence teaser + "Read more" link
  2. Customer spotlight: Brief story or testimonial
  3. Product highlight: New or popular offering + benefit
  4. Exclusive offer: Special for newsletter subscribers
  5. Resource: Free download, guide, video, etc.
- Tone: Helpful, community-focused, not overly salesy
- Includes: Mix of content (tips, news, products, community)
- CTA buttons: One per section, clear action
- Footer: Social links, unsubscribe, brand contact
- Length: 300-500 words total
- Visuals: Descriptions of images/icons for each section
```

### Prompt 3.5: Holiday/Seasonal Email
```
Write a holiday [HOLIDAY] promotional email for [BRAND] that:
- Subject line: Holiday cheer + incentive (under 55 characters)
  - Convey: Holiday spirit, special offer, urgency
- Opening: Seasonal greeting and emotional connection
- The offer: Specific discount, bundle, or special deal
  - Discount %, free shipping, gift with purchase, etc.
- Duration: Clear end date (e.g., "through December 24th")
- Featured products: 3-5 gift ideas relevant to your customers
- Social proof: "Bestselling gift," "Highly rated," customer reviews
- Urgency: Limited quantities, limited time
- CTA: "Shop [Holiday] Deals" or "Get [Discount]%Off Now"
- Trust elements: Return policy, guarantee, secure checkout
- Tone: Warm, festive, generous, excited

- Consider: Mobile optimization (many check email on phones)
```

## SECTION 4: SALES & CONVERSION PROMPTS

### Prompt 4.1: Landing Page Headlines
```
Generate 8 alternative landing page headlines for [PRODUCT/SERVICE] that:
- Each targets [TARGET AUDIENCE] differently
- Avoid generic claims like "Best Product Ever"
- Follow these 8 different angles:
  1. Benefit-driven: "You'll [SPECIFIC BENEFIT] with [PRODUCT]"
  2. Problem-solving: "Never [PAIN POINT] again"
  3. Curiosity-creating: "What [INDUSTRY EXPERTS] don't tell you about [TOPIC]"
  4. Social proof: "[IMPRESSIVE STAT] people already [BENEFIT]"
  5. Exclusive/VIP: "The [PRODUCT] only [PEOPLE] know about"
  6. Fear-based: "Don't let [CONSEQUENCE] happen to you"
  7. Transformation: "From [BEFORE STATE] to [AFTER STATE] in [TIMEFRAME]"
  8. Value proposition: "[PRODUCT] does [BENEFIT] so you don't have to [PAIN]"
- Each headline: Under 10 words
- Use power words: [POWER WORDS - fast, easy, free, proven, exclusive, etc.]
- Tone: Compelling and credible (not hype-y)
- All headlines should pass the "Would you click this?" test
```

### Prompt 4.2: CTA Button Text
```
Generate 15 high-converting call-to-action button texts for [ACTION - buying/subscribing/downloading/signing up] that:
- Vary in tone: urgent, benefit-driven, curiosity-creating, comfort-focused
- Use strong action verbs: Get, Claim, Discover, Start, Join, Unlock, Grab, Try
- Create clear expectation: User knows exactly what will happen when they click
- Vary in length:
  - 2-word power: "Get Started," "Claim Now"
  - 3-4 word specificity: "Get Your Free Trial," "Start 30-Day Free"
  - 5+ word benefit: "Yes, Show Me How," "I Want [BENEFIT]"
- Include these tones:
  - Urgent: "Claim Now," "Don't Wait," "Limited Spots"
  - Benefit-focused: "Get My [BENEFIT]," "Start Saving"
  - Curiosity: "Show Me How," "Reveal My Results"
  - Comfort: "Try Risk-Free," "No Credit Card Needed"
  - Exclusive: "Join the [GROUP]," "Get Access Now"
- Avoid: "Click Here," "Submit," generic phrases
- Consider: What objection does each button overcome?
```

### Prompt 4.3: Sales Page Copy (Long Form)
```
Write sales page copy for [PRODUCT/SERVICE] (target: [AUDIENCE]) that:
- Structure:
  1. HEADLINE: Address primary benefit, hook attention
  2. SUBHEADING: Expand on headline, create desire
  3. OPENING STORY: 1-2 paragraph origin story or customer scenario
  4. PROBLEM SECTION: Validate customer pain, "You're not alone"
  5. SOLUTION INTRO: Present your product as the answer
  6. FEATURES → BENEFITS: 5-7 bullets, each showing feature + benefit
  7. PROOF SECTION: Testimonials, case studies, success stories, statistics
  8. ADDRESSING OBJECTIONS: FAQ format, handle common "buts"
  9. GUARANTEE/RISK REVERSAL: Your promise to customer
  10. CLOSING STATEMENT: Emotional appeal + sense of urgency
  11. MULTIPLE CTAs: Button placements throughout
- Total length: [WORD COUNT - typically 1000-2000 words]
- Tone: Conversational yet confident, never salesy
- Includes: Social proof, credibility markers, clear benefits
- Emphasize: Result/outcome, not features
```

### Prompt 4.4: High-Ticket Sales Email
```
Write a sales email for [HIGH-TICKET PRODUCT/SERVICE] (price: $[PRICE]) for [TARGET DECISION-MAKER] that:
- Subject line: Professional, intriguing, not salesy (under 50 characters)
- Greeting: Personalized to recipient's interests/role
- Opening: Acknowledge what they care about, specific compliment or observation
- Problem: State the challenge [TARGET] faces specifically
- Solution intro: Your product/service as the answer
- ROI/Value: Quantify the benefit (save $X, gain Y hours, achieve Z metric)
- Proof: Success stories with similar companies/clients
- Why now: Create mild urgency without pressure
- Next step: Propose specific meeting type/call time (not vague "let's talk")
- Close: Professional, confident, shows respect for their time
- Signature: Your name, title, company, direct contact info
- Tone: Peer-to-peer, respectful, consultative (not salesy)
- Length: 150-200 words (short and impactful)
- Follow-up: Note when you'll follow up if no response
```
